CodeScent — the quality-aware navigator

Hand the model the smallest, most relevant, most trustworthy slice of your codebase — so its tokens go to thinking, not searching.

Heart: Quality-aware navigator Rule: Trustworthy facts + optional smart layer Network: open Sources: fff · cbm · LLM Date: 2026-06-29

1What's settled

North star

CodeScent is the LLM's navigator for your codebase. It finds the right code, knows which code is healthy or risky, and feeds the model a tight, trustworthy bundle — fewer tokens, sharper focus.

One scarce resource sits under everything: the model's context window. Tokens cost money, junk dilutes attention, and compaction erases memory. Every decision below serves that one truth.

1

A navigator, not an engine. CodeScent doesn't out-search fff or out-graph cbm — it drives them, plus a native floor and an LLM layer, and bounds the result. Orchestration is the product.

2

Quality-awareness is the moat. fff and cbm find code; neither knows if it's any good. CodeScent's smell/health work stops being a separate product and becomes the ranking + safety signal that makes the navigator smarter than either. Inspector serves Navigator.

3

Trustworthy facts vs probabilistic judgment. Facts (where is X, who calls Y, what's the smell, the CI gate) stay deterministic — always the floor and the fallback. Judgment (rank, summarize, suggest, understand vague asks) can use the LLM: opt-in, cached, labeled. This replaces "offline" — network is fine; just don't let guesses poison the facts.

4

One yardstick for every decision. Does it get the right code into the model with fewer tokens and tighter focus? Yes → build it. No → cut it. Borrow-mode, API-vs-sampling, every "should we?" answers to this ruler.

5

Cheap token wins before engines. Tighten the answers we already give (native, no deps) before wiring fff, cbm, or the LLM layer. Instant payoff, zero risk.

↺ What this demotes (not deletes)

The heavy Inspector apparatus — full findings lifecycle, calibration, SARIF, the CI health ratchet — stays as an optional module and a source of ranking signal. It just stops driving the roadmap. The navigation mission leads; quality feeds it.

4Why this order

  • Measure first (0). You can't manage token spend you don't see; the benchmark makes every later win provable.
  • Cheapest wins next (1). Native, zero-dep payload tightening touches every tool — instant payoff before any engine risk.
  • Fix finding before ranking (2 → 3). Fast, forgiving retrieval is the floor; quality-aware ranking is the layer that makes it smart — and it's your moat, so it gets its own phase.
  • Intelligence rides on top (4). Semantic + LLM rerank are most valuable once retrieval and quality signals exist to rank and fall back to. Network opens here, deliberately and contained.
  • Prove, then dream (5 → M). The benchmark must show the core paying off before XL effort on the autonomous planner or compound ranking.

5Monday move & guardrails

▶ What to do first

Benchmark harness + Phase 1 auto_expand_defs. Capture the token baseline, ship the def-expansion, watch the count drop on a real session. Proof and momentum in one move.

⚠ Guardrails (the kernel that survived "offline")

  • Facts stay deterministic. Never let an LLM call into scan_code_health or the CI gate — that breaks reproducibility and calibration. LLM augments, never replaces, the fact layer.
  • LLM layer is opt-in, cached, labeled. Every probabilistic result is marked "subjective" and falls back to deterministic when the call is off or fails.
  • Stay bounded. Every answer keeps its bound + result-handle. The moment CodeScent returns unbounded payloads, it's just another engine.
  • Engines stay optional. Remove fff, cbm, or the network and the navigator still works on native alone.
